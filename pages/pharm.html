Pharmacovigilance and Medical Regulatory Writing

**Overview of Pharmacovigilance and Medical Regulatory Writing** Pharmacovigilance (PV), or drug safety, involves the detection, assessment, understanding, and prevention of adverse effects related to medications, ensuring their safety and effectiveness from development to post-marketing. It encompasses aspects like adverse event detection, risk management, regulatory compliance, and post-marketing surveillance, playing a vital role in patient safety and public health. Medical regulatory writing within pharmacovigilance and clinical trials entails producing various technical documents to meet regulatory standards and communicate vital safety information. This includes clinical study protocols, clinical study reports (CSR), informed consent documents, investigator's brochures (IB), safety narratives, and applications for new drug or marketing authorizations (NDA/MAA).
